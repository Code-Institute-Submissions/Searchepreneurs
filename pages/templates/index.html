SEO is short for 'search engine optimization'. Sites that are better optimized in this way will show up more often in search results, leading to a larger number of people visiting the site (after all, anyone who uses google can be a potential customer to a well optimized site.

What's important to remember with SEO is that it's not as complicated as you might think, and it's a long term solution, which might take up to 5 months to take effect.

SEO is the king of customer aquisition for a number of reasons. For one, it's mostly free and when it does cost you, it's dirt cheap! SEO audits or SEO apps are nothing compared to the cost of PPC (pay per click) advertising or other methods of customer aquisition.

In addition, SEO tends to have a high conversion rate, as the users coming to your site are coming from searchingfor what you're selling. Conversion rates from search results can easily exceed 5%, a stunning number when compared to the industry average of ~2%.

Finally, bad SEO practices can cripple your site. Poor loading times are a good example of this. If you look at the graph below, you'll see that if your site's load time exceeds 5 seconds, more than 1 in 5 of your customers are going to leave before even looking at your site:

Over the years, search engine algorithms have become more and more sophisticated. For sites with good SEO practices, this means that their hard work is rewarded with swathes of new customers.

For sites who don't adapt and optimize, however, it means being buried on the 50th page of any search result if you show up at all.

Some examples of good SEO practices are:

  • Quick load speeds
  • No broken links on your site
  • A healthy mixture of links to the site, within the site, and directing out of the site.
